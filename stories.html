Reason

We both had reasons. I decided to stay while you decided to run away. You were fed up while I was wondering we should be together more. How can I be reasonable? To me our love was everything and you were my whole life. It is not very pleasant to realize that to you it was only an episode. And I always feel sorry for you. I'm always sorry because I hurt you. You wanted to leave but I always followed you. It's something I must live with every day. And all the pain I put you through, I wish that I could take it all away and be the one who catches all your tears. Be the one who loves you cares you and protect you. But you’re already fed up with me. You had the reasons to stay away from me, to live a new life happily. I had the reason to stay away from you, to make you happy and live my own life as if it’s a curse. The sad part is, that I will probably end up loving you without you for much longer than I loved you when I knew you. Some people might find that strange. But the truth of it is that the amount of love you feel for someone and the impact they have on you as a person is in no way relative to the amount of time you have known them. -Pankaj Ojha

Song

It was dusk time and a women was singing. Her song reminded me of something which take me back to those years. The time when I was playing guitar. Life was awesome back then. I still remember how much I enjoy when I get tingles while playing the strings. And those little pains on the tip of my fingers while shifting the chords. Her voice also reminded me of the mysterious song which my momma used to sing back then. In the cold dark Saturday nights where I used to hear her sitting aside a fire. Those were the days. Now it’s a vain remembering those songs. Now the song is down in the flood of remembrance, and I smile for the past I had. Wonderful Days. -Prashant Subedi

Happiness

In today’s world, even though we may be fully conscious of the spiritual emptiness of material attainment, we still need reminders that fulfillment has nothing to do with having possessions. People certainly try the strangest option for attaining fulfillment. They may drug themselves into ignorance. They may alter their bodies to achieve some arbitrary standard of beauty. They may work their whole lives to reach the pinnacle of success, only to have it mercilessly pulled from them in a second. Every decision we make, at some level, boils down to trying to be happy. But most sensible people know that there are no easy routes to long term happiness. If you place your bets on temporary pleasures, you will find only temporary satisfaction. With cheap thrills, you get what you pay for –here today, and gone tomorrow. Life isn’t about having, it’s about being. You could surround yourself with all that money can buy, and you’d still be as miserable as a human can be. When you look for happiness in mere objects, they are never enough. Look around, look within. True happiness comes gradually, through the sum of your life choices—of yesterday, but also today and tomorrow.
-Puspa Subedi
